Cultural Heritage
Royal Beauty Traditions of Joseon
For over 500 years, the royal court of Joseon Dynasty preserved sacred beauty rituals that embodied
the Korean philosophy of natural harmony and inner radiance.
These time-honored traditions represent not merely cosmetic practices, but a profound cultural heritage
that celebrates the eternal pursuit of beauty through nature's wisdom.

Gyuhap Chongseo
The Encyclopedia of Women's Wisdom
In 1809, the remarkable Lady Bingheo (憑虛閣 李氏) completed what would become the most comprehensive guide to Korean women's wisdom: the Gyuhap Chongseo (閨閤叢書).
"Within these pages lie the secrets of radiant skin achieved through natural harmony - the very principles that guide our formulations today."
This extraordinary 4-volume encyclopedia contained everything from royal beauty formulations to seasonal skincare rituals, preserving centuries of knowledge that might otherwise have been lost to time.
Cultural Legacy
"Beauty in Korean culture has always been inseparable from virtue, health, and harmony with nature.
The Gyuhap Chongseo stands as testament to this philosophy — a sacred text that preserved not merely
beauty formulations, but a profound way of living that honors both inner cultivation and outer expression."
Living Heritage
Today, we are the guardians of this extraordinary legacy. Every formulation honors the wisdom of
Lady Bingheo, every ritual connects us to centuries of Korean women who understood that true beauty emanates
from the harmony between natural ingredients and mindful practice.
